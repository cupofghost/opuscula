# OPVSCVLA

Thirty small musical machines. Each is a single self-contained HTML file of pure
Web Audio — no build, no dependencies, no samples, no trackers. The machine
composes; you set the law it obeys.

**Live:** https://cupofghost.github.io/opuscula/

| XV | [DIAMOND](./diamond/) | Harry Partch's tonality diamond — named in English, Partch's own tongue and his own word for the instrument and the lattice alike. Partch tuned everything from a single 1/1 (his was G, 392 Hz, kept here) and arranged every ratio of the identities 1/3/5/7/9/11 over one another in a diamond-shaped grid: read down a column and you get an *otonality*, a slice of the harmonic series — bright, major-rooted; read across a row and you get its subharmonic mirror, a *utonality* — dark, minor-rooted, the chord no tempered instrument can reach. | XVII | [TESSERA](./tessera/) | A machine that predicts itself — named in Latin (*tessera*: the token, the mosaic tile, the soldier's watchword). It carries the smallest real language model — a variable-order Markov model with interpolated PPM-C blending — that starts **empty**, knows nothing, and composes by predicting itself: read the context, form a distribution over what comes next, sample it at a temperature, commit, and let the choice become context. It learns only from its own output, and — because its memory is a finite window — it also *forgets*, and is surprised by its own dissolved habits when they return. The one aesthetic channel a next-token predictor and a human ear genuinely share is expectation (Meyer 1956, Huron's ITPRA, IDyOM), so the model's exact per-note **surprisal** (−log₂ p) and **entropy** drive everything you hear: VOX sings the chosen note (surprise brightens and sharpens it); NIMBVS is an audible probability halo of the notes that could have been; PVLSVS fidgets when the mind is uncertain; FVNDVS holds a just-intonation ground. Designed, in answer to the question of what machine an LLM would like, by one. |

| XIX | [TRITAVA](./tritava/) | A music with no octave — named in Latin (*tritava*, the coined word *tritave* Latinised). Nearly every scale on earth repeats when a frequency doubles, at the octave (2:1); the **Bohlen–Pierce scale** repeats instead at the *tritave*, the perfect twelfth (3:1), and builds its thirteen just-tuned steps from the odd harmonics 3, 5 and 7 — so the octave, the most basic interval in all other music, never occurs. Derived by Heinz Bohlen (c. 1972) and rediscovered by John R. Pierce and Max Mathews, who showed its chords only ring consonant on tones made of *odd* harmonics; so every voice here — reed drone, glass bells, a clarinet-voiced melody, a soft tick — is synthesized from odd harmonics only, the spectrum in which this scale means anything. It uses the exact *just* Bohlen–Pierce ratios (1/1, 27/25, 25/21, 9/7, 7/5, 75/49, 5/3, 9/5, 49/25, 15/7, 7/3, 63/25, 25/9, 3/1), not the equal-tempered version — no equal temperament anywhere. Choose the mode (Lambda, the primary diatonic · Gamma · the six-note Harmonia · full Chromatic), the motion and the meter; the machine walks a seeded progression of Bohlen–Pierce chords (3:5:7 and 5:7:9) home to the tonic each phrase, and draws the whole scale as a thirteen-spoke wheel that closes at the tritave where an octave-repeating scale would have closed an octave earlier. Voices fold between registers by *tritaves*, never octaves. |
| XX | [GERMEN](./germen/) | A machine that grows music from grammar — the plant is the score. Four species — FRONDA (a fern), SALIX (a willow), ALGA (Lindenmayer's own 1968 original), PAVIMENTUM (the Gosper flowsnake, a plane-tiling curve) — each grow a string that a turtle walks *twice at once*: as a drawing (forward, turn, branch) and as a score, where every forward step is a plucked Karplus–Strong string, every turn raises or lowers the scale degree, and every bracketed side-branch is a little melody that leaves the main stem untouched, higher and quicker the deeper it nests. What you see light up on the plant is the note you hear. |
| XXI | [FORFEX](./forfex/) | Music made by cutting tape — named in Latin (*forfex*, the shears). Before samplers, the first electronic composers built pieces with a razor blade: record a sound to magnetic tape, then physically cut and splice it into a new order. This machine is that studio, with every raw sound synthesized rather than recorded. It generates a small library of sources (sine mixtures, noise bands, a struck body, breath, a formant voice, a click), then a seeded editor cuts them into segments and splices them onto a reel — some sped up or slowed to a higher or lower pitch (*varispeed*, always an exact just ratio, never equal temperament), some reversed, a bed looped underneath. Four **Studios** set the school and its habits: Paris (musique concrète, free montage, wide leaps), Köln (elektronische Musik, pure tones and noise bands, serial pitch order), Radiophonique (the BBC workshop's mixed palette), and Phase (two copies of one loop drifting out of phase and back, after Reich and Riley). **Montage** sets how fast the cuts come, **Cut** chooses a hard butt splice or a crossfaded diagonal one, and **Wear** ages the whole reel — wow, flutter, magnetic saturation and hiss, modelled and printed into the bake. The plate draws two reels turning above the reel laid flat as an edit strip, every splice a razor line. |

| XXIII | [RICERCAR](./ricercar/) | The riddle canons of Bach's *Musical Offering*, run as a formal system — named in Latin, *ricercar*, "to seek," and an acrostic in Bach's own dedication (*Regis Iussu Cantio Et Reliqua Canonica Arte Resoluta*). In 1747 Frederick the Great handed the visiting Bach a chromatic theme and asked for an improvised fugue; Bach went home and mailed back ten canons on it, several as *riddle canons* — a single notated line and a Latin motto, the second voice left for the performer to find. This machine does what the riddle asks: your seed is spelled, in base twelve, into six notes of the royal theme (Gödel numbering — the URL is audibly in the tune, bijective both ways), then a deterministic search looks for canons — retrograde, mirror-inverted, augmented, endlessly modulating — that verify as contrapuntally true against the theme, honestly relaxing its own rules (marked *licentia*) rather than hanging when no clean solution turns up in time. The finale, the *canon per tonos*, rises a whole tone through six real, differently-coloured **Werckmeister III** keys (A=415, Bach's own well temperament, exact) and folds itself down an octave at the seam to restart the piece — Hofstadter's *strange loop* made audible, with a deliberately unprovable B–A–C–H sounding across the fold. The canvas draws each canon's own geometry: the crab canon as a palindrome, the mirror canon as *Drawing Hands*, the modulating finale as a closed spiral of six flights. |
| XXIV | [SVARA](./svara/) | South Indian Carnatic music — svara over the twenty-two shrutis, named in Sanskrit (*svara*, from *svayaṁ rājate*, "that which shines of itself," the musical note itself). | XXV | [ŠIYÓTȞAŊKA](./siyotanka/) | The Lakota courting flute — named in Lakota, *šiyótȟaŋka*, the "big prairie-chicken flute." ## Shared grammar

- **space** = play/stop · **p** = pause/resume · **r** = another (aliud/encore) · **c** = cut 16-bit WAV
- Every work carries an expandable **“on this music”** panel — a short, plain-language
  history of the musical idea it renders.
- **Change it while it plays.** Where a work runs a groove — Rille and Scala, and
  Peal’s key and speed — the controls take effect on the fly: the music keeps
  running and re-vibes at the next bar rather than starting over.
- **The URL hash is the pressing.** Every parameter — seed included — serializes
  into `#…`. Copy the address and the exact opus reloads anywhere.
- Offline render: each work cuts a deterministic WAV of itself.
- iOS: audio requests the `playback` session (ignores the silent switch on
  Safari 17+); resumes across interruptions.

## Backstage: OFFICINA, the voicing bench

Not an op. — the workshop. Every machine keeps its synthesized voices in one
documented `TIMBRE` block near the top of its script (every constant named,
bounded, and explained), and [`officina/`](./officina/) edits those values
**live inside the running machine**: pick a work, press its play control, and
shape the voices by ear in the mix they actually sit in. Save what you dial in
as named presets (per browser; export/import as JSON); the machines pick up the
applied voicing even when opened directly. `?factory` on any machine's URL
plays the shipped voice. Voicing changes timbre only — seeds, hash pressings,
and the composition are untouched.
