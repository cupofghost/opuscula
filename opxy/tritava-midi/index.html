2 · pitch-bend / channel routing

Bohlen–Pierce repeats at the tritave (3/1), not the octave — an octave-repeating tuning table can't hold it. So each note is sent on its own MIDI channel and bent to its exact frequency. One note per channel = independent tuning per voice (the MPE idea).

⚠ the OP–XY's pitch-bend range (system settings → MIDI → pitchbend) must equal the value above, or every bent note will land at the wrong pitch. ±2 semitones is a safe, precise default.

5 · MIDI stream

on this fork

Path B is for the machines the tuning table can't express: more than 12 pitches per octave, non-octave scales, or tunings that drift while they play. TRITAVA is the sharpest case — the Bohlen–Pierce scale is built from the odd harmonics 3·5·7 and closes at the tritave (3/1, a perfect twelfth), so it has no octave at all. You cannot fold it into a per-octave detune table; the pitches simply aren't there.

The fix is per-note pitch bend. For every note the fork finds the nearest 12-TET key, computes the cents it must bend to reach the exact BP frequency, puts the note on its own channel, and sends the bend before the note-on. With MPE routing that's up to 15 independently-tuned voices at once. This is where "MPE is needed" is literally true.

The open risk: whether the OP–XY honours voice-per-channel MPE input is unconfirmed. The poly-channel pool is the fallback — it works on any multitimbral synth that applies pitch bend per channel, capped at the pool size. Set the device's bend range to match, and the monitor (playing the exact frequencies) is your reference for whether the device agrees.
