On this bench · how voicing works here

What this is

Organ builders call it voicing: the pipes are built, the music is written, and then someone stands at the instrument and adjusts each rank's speech — brighter, darker, more breath, less bite — until it sings in the room it lives in. This page is that bench for the opuscula. It is backstage machinery, not an op.; the catalogue's music is unchanged by its existence.

Where the sound actually lives

Each machine's source carries a TIMBRE block near the top of its script — every synthesis constant, named, bounded, and documented, grouped by voice. The machine's code reads those values instead of scattered magic numbers, so the numbers on this bench are the instrument's voice. The bench talks to the machine over postMessage while it plays; nothing here is a mock-up or a preview — the frame on the left is the real machine, real scheduler, real mix.

Presets

Edits write to this browser's storage as the machine's applied voice: open the machine directly — even from the catalogue — and it speaks with your voicing. Name and save any state as a preset; load, overwrite, or delete them here; export them as JSON to move between devices or to hand back for baking into the factory voice. FACTORY returns the shipped sound (the machine also obeys a ?factory URL switch). The A/B button flips the running machine to the factory voice while lit — the honest way to hear what your tweaks are doing.

Determinism, unharmed

Seeds, URL-hash pressings, and the offline WAV cut all still reproduce exactly — voicing changes timbre, never the composition. But a WAV cut while a preset is applied is cut in that preset's voice: what you hear is what you get.
